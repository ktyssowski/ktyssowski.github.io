I am a BRAIN Initiative K99/R00 Postdoctoral Fellow in the [Hoekstra Lab](https://hoekstra.oeb.harvard.edu/) at Harvard University where I am using arboreal and non-arboreal deer mice (*Peromyscus maniculatus*) as a comparative system to uncover the neural basis of the evolution of skilled motor behavior.

I recieved my PhD in 2019 from the [Biological and Biomecial Sciences](https://www.hms.harvard.edu/dms/bbs/) program at Harvard University. In my [dissertation project](https://dash.harvard.edu/handle/1/42029792), I investigated the relationship between neuronal activity pattern and activity-regulated gene expression. Before grad school, I was a reserch technician in the [Gotoh Lab](http://www.f.u-tokyo.ac.jp/~molbio/english/e_index.html) at the University of Tokyo.
